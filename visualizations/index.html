CPSC583 Hand-In 3 Visualizations

Creating 9 visualizations of the Google Play App Store, our sketch, shown from the report put link to report here!!! was to visualize how the different genres and price points interact. Playing with scales, colors, graph types and more, we were able to experiment on how difference in graphs make more differences in understanding. Each graph will be labelled with the changes done to them from the initial graph.

Our main visualization

Created a simple bar chart showing the minimum and maximum price points of apps in each genre.

Variation 1

We group up the genres even further, placing them into broader genres defined by the App store. Hence, all of the specific game genres (Action, Adventure, Brain, etc) are now placed into Games.

Variation 2

Due to several apps having extremely high price points, we utilize a log scale in order to better fit our graph. In the linear scale, it was difficult to see the price range of genres with inexpensive apps. Here, it is clear to see that certain genres have no paid apps, since they have no bars.

Variation 3

Still utilizing our log scale, we now convert to a scatter plot to see a point for every app, instead of just being able to see the maximum and minimum. One can see the range of apps with slightly differing prices. However, for the free apps, we see that they are overlapping. Thus, we cannot tell if there are 1 or 1000 apps that are free in a particular genre.

Variation 4

A colored bar chart with log scaling. Different colors represent different price ranges.

Variation 5

A colored scatter graph with log scaling. Different colors represent different price ranges. The color scale changes for every $50 increase in app price beyond an initial range of $0-10.

Variation 6

A line graph with linear scaling, representing the same information as our main concept.

Variation 7

Utilizing line segments per price point, instead of points like our scatter plot.

Variation 8

Heated Scatter plot where colors and point radius represent the number of applications that have a certain price. White represents a single app that has a specific price. The more blue and large a circle is, the more applications have the corresponding price.

Variation 9

Heated bar segment graph, utilizing the color red this time in order to better standout from the blue background. The intensity of the color red represents the number of applications that have a particular price.
