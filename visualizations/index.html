CPSC583 Hand-In 3 Visualizations

Creating 9 visualizations of the Google Play App Store, our sketch, shown from the report put link to report here!!! was to visualize how the different genres and price points interact. Playing with scales, colors, graph types and more, we were able to experiment on how difference in graphs make more differences in understanding. Each graph will be labelled with the changes done to them from the initial graph with links to all of them.

Our main visualization

Created a simple bar chart with all of the genres from the min to max price points.

Variation 1

We group up the genres even further, placing them into the App genres. Hence, all of the different games (Action, Adventure, Brain, etc) are now placed into Games.

Variation 2

Due to several apps having extremely high price points, we utilize a log scale in order to better fit our graph. It is clear to see the free apps with no bars.

Variation 3

Still utilizing our log scale, we now convert to a scatter plot to see all the different points, instead of just being able to see the max and min. One can see all the dots with slightly differing prices, however for the free apps, we see that they are overlapping hence cannot tell if there is 1 or 1000 apps that are free.

Variation 4

A colored bar chart with log scaling. Different colors represent different price ranges.

Variation 5

A colored scatter graph with log scaling. Different colors represent different price ranges.

Variation 6

A line graph with linear scaling, representing the same information as our main concept.

Variation 7

Utilizing line segments per price point, instead of points.

Variation 8

Heated Scatter plot graph where colors represent the number of applications in that range. White represents a single app, while the the more blue and larger the circle is, the more applications there are.

Variation 9

Heated bar segment graph, utilizing the color red this time in order to better standout from the blue background. The intensity of the color red represents the number of applications.
